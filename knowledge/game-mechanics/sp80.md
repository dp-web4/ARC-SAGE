# sp80 — Gravity Pipes: Position Pipes to Channel Falling Water into Receptacles

## Toolset
Available actions: [ACTION1=UP, ACTION2=DOWN, ACTION3=LEFT, ACTION4=RIGHT, ACTION5=POUR, ACTION6=CLICK].
Click selects pipe segments. Arrows reposition them. ACTION5 triggers the pour/spill simulation. This is a liquid-flow puzzle with a rotated display.

## Objects
- **Pipe segments** (tags `ksmzdcblcz`, `sys_click`): Horizontal bars of color 8 (grey), various widths (3-8 cells). Named `jgfvrvnkaz` (5w), `odioorqnkn` (6w), `trurgcakbj` (4w), `untfxhpddv` (3w), `nvzozwqarf` (8w), `mdhkebfsmg` (1w x 4h vertical), `uihgaxtzkm` (7w). Movable obstacles that redirect water flow.
- **L-shaped/angled pipes** (tags `hfjpeygkxy`, `sys_click`): `qwsmjdrvqj` and `vkwijvqdla` (2x2 L-shapes, color 15/white). These deflect water flow 90 degrees.
- **Drip source** (`syaipsfndp`): 1x1 color 4 pixel. Water drips from below this point.
- **Pour-point marker** (`adbrqflmwi`): 7-wide bar with center pixel color 4. Has tag `syaipsfndp` -- also acts as a drip source.
- **Water drops** (`nkrtlkykwe`): 1x1 color 6 (pink) pixels. Start at position (source_x, 1). Spread downward in the spill simulation.
- **Receptacles** (`xsrqllccpx`): 3x2 shapes, color 11 (cyan). Tagged `xsrqllccpx`. These are the targets that must be filled. Turn color 13 (magenta) when properly filled.
- **Danger zone** (`uzunfxpwmd`): 32-wide bar, color 1 (dark blue). Tagged `uzunfxpwmd`. Water hitting this = failure (turns color 14, triggers flash animation).
- **Walls/border**: `uzvelihpxo` (18x18) and `ttkatugvbk` (22x22) and `nadtnzkesz` (34x34) -- border frames of color 1.
- **Step counter UI**: Progress bar on row 0, color 14 (filled) vs 0 (empty).
- **Rotation indicator UI** (`hbwuwfezbg`): Rotates the entire rendered frame by 0/90/180/270 degrees. The display is physically rotated.

## Interaction Model
- **Rotation system**: The entire game display is rotated by `rotation` degrees (from level data). All inputs are remapped: arrow keys and click coordinates are transformed to match the rotated view. `wdxitozphu` maps actions for rotations 1-3 (90, 180, 270 deg).
- **CLICK (ACTION6)**: After coordinate rotation transform, finds pipe/L-pipe at grid position. Selects it (turns color 9/maroon, layer 1). Re-clicking or clicking empty deselects.
- **Arrows (ACTION1-4)**: Moves selected pipe by 1 cell. Validates: y >= 3 (not above display), no collision with receptacles (with 1-cell buffer). Pipes can overlap other pipes.
- **POUR (ACTION5)**: Triggers the spill simulation. Max 4 pours per game (5th pour = lose). Steps:
  1. Creates water drops below each drip source pixel (color 4 on `syaipsfndp` tagged sprites).
  2. Water propagates frame-by-frame: each drop moves in its current direction.
  3. **On hitting a pipe** (`ksmzdcblcz`): water splits to both perpendicular sides.
  4. **On hitting a receptacle** (`xsrqllccpx`): if water approaches from both perpendicular sides simultaneously, the receptacle fills (turns color 13). Otherwise water splashes around.
  5. **On hitting an L-pipe** (`hfjpeygkxy`): water deflects 90 degrees (direction changes based on which side it hits).
  6. **On hitting danger zone** (`uzunfxpwmd`): FAIL -- the zone turns color 14, triggers flash animation, then resets (cleanup, decrements remaining pours).
  7. Simulation ends when no more active water drops. If all receptacles are filled AND no danger zone was hit, level is won.
- **After failed pour**: all temporary water drops are removed, receptacles reset to color 11, danger zones reset to color 1, step counter decremented. Player repositions pipes and tries again.

## Win Condition
After spill simulation completes: check if ALL receptacles (`xsrqllccpx`) are in the `srwrqoodsc` (filled) set AND no danger zone was hit (`enlvswjeov` is False). **Win = all receptacles filled without hitting the danger zone.** Then `next_level()` is called.

## Level Progression
**6 levels**. Grid sizes: 16x16 (levels 1-3), 20x20 (levels 4-6). Changes:
- **Level 1**: 1 pipe, 1 drip source, 2 receptacles, 30 steps, rotation=0.
- **Level 2**: 2 small pipes, 2 drip sources, 3 receptacles, 45 steps, rotation=180 (upside down).
- **Level 3**: 4 pipes, 3 drip sources, 3 receptacles, 100 steps, rotation=180.
- **Level 4**: 3 pipes + 1 pour-point marker, 1 drip source, 4 receptacles, 120 steps, rotation=0. Larger grid.
- **Level 5**: 2 pipes + 1 L-pipe, 2 drip sources, 4 receptacles (one rotated 270), 100 steps, rotation=180. Side danger zones.
- **Level 6**: 1 vertical pipe + 2 L-pipes + 1 pour-point, 1 drip source, 4 receptacles (rotated), 120 steps, rotation=0. Three side danger zones.

Key data per level: `steps` (int), `rotation` (0/90/180/270).

## No-Source Discovery Strategy
1. **First action**: Click on a grey bar shape. Observe it highlights (turns maroon).
2. **Observe**: Note drip source at top, receptacles at bottom, danger zone at very bottom. Arrow-move the pipe.
3. **Hypothesis**: Press ACTION5 to pour. Water falls from the source and interacts with pipes.
4. **Test**: Position pipe under the drip source, pour, observe water splitting left and right along the pipe. Move pipes to guide water into receptacles.
5. **When ready**: Plan pipe placement so water from each source reaches each receptacle without hitting the danger zone. Remember: display may be rotated (check if gravity seems to go in an unexpected direction).

## Key Patterns
- Display rotation completely transforms the visual. Rotation=180 means everything appears upside down; water still "falls down" in game coords but looks like it rises on screen.
- Max 4 pours. Each failed pour costs 1 attempt.
- L-pipes deflect water 90 degrees. Straight pipes split water perpendicular to flow direction.
- Receptacles need water from BOTH sides simultaneously to fill (the perpendicular adjacency check).
- Step counter decrements by 1 per non-reset action in "change" mode.
- Coordinate formula: for rotation k, display coords are transformed via `udeubouzyp()` using 63-based complement.
